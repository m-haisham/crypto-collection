Hamming Code

Hamming code is a set of error-correction codes that are used to detect and correct errors that may occur during the transmission of the data from the sender to the receiver.

The technique was developed by R.W. Hamming.

Luhn Algorithm

Luhn algorithm or Luhn formula is a simple checksum formula used to validate a variety of identification numbers such as, credit card numbers and IMEI numbers.

It was named after its creator, IBM scientist Hans Peter Luhn.

Password Cracking

Password cracking refers to various methods and measures used to recover data that has been stored in or transmitted by a computer system.

Repeatedly trying guesses for the password and to check them against an already available hashing algorithm is a common approach. This is also known a brute-force attack.

A variant of the above mentioned is the dictionary attack. It uses lists of words most likely or lists of passwords obtained from previous security breaches as opposed to trying every possible combination of letters.
