A brute-force attack uses trial-and-error to guess the passwords or passphrases. It is the digital equivalent of trying every key in your key ring, and eventually finding the right one.

A brute-force attack can, in theory, be used to decrypt any encrypted key. However in practice this is not feasible. For longer passwords such an attack may take years to guess, as the length of the password increases the number possible combinations increase exponentially.

However for shorter password it is still a simple and reliable method to decrypt a password. And for longer passwords other methods like dictionary attack are used.
