Luhn algorithm or Luhn formula is a simple checksum formula used to validate a variety of identification numbers such as, credit card numbers and IMEI numbers.

It was named after its creator, IBM scientist Hans Peter Luhn.

The validity of the credit card number is determined by a check digit calculated by the following algorithm to calculate the check digit. wiki

  1. Take the original number and starting from the rightmost digit moving left, double the value of every second digit (including the rightmost digit).
  2. Replace the resulting value at each position with the sum of the digits of this position's value.
    • 12 = 1 + 2 = 3
    • 18 = 1 + 8 = 9
  3. Sum up the resulting values from all positions (s).
  4. The calculated check digit is equal to 10 - s mod 10.

Below are some other information that can be extracted from the credit card number.

  • Major Industry Identifier: The first digit of a credit card number represent the category of entity which issued the card.
  • Issuer identification number: The first six digits of a card number identify the institution that issued the card.
  • Personal Account identifier: From digit no. 7 to final minus 1, indicate individual account identifier and the The last digit is the checksum number.
